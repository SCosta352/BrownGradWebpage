Notation and Structure

Once the single sample of size \(n\) is collected from the population of interest, counts of observations are summarized in a contingency table.

The General Social Survey

The General Social Survey (GSS) is a survey conducted by NORC, a research organization housed at the University of Chicago. According to the webpage, the goal of the research is “to monitor Americans’ shifting attitudes on social issues…[and] to provide clear, unbiased data on what Americans think about important topics such as civil liberties, intergroup relations, and gender roles.” We can read in a portion of the survey data using the following command:

gss <- read.csv("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lchihara/MathStatsResamplingR/main/Edition2/Data/GSS2002.csv")

The survey contains information on a number of different variables. We will investigate the association between an individual’s opinion on whether or not a permit should be required to buy a gun (GunLaw) and highest level of education (’Education`).

The command below allows us to create a contingency table summarizing counts of observations for these two variables. The first line of code creates the table of counts categorized by the factors and saves it as mytable; the second adds row and column sums to the table.

mytable <- table(gss$Education,gss$GunLaw)
addmargins(mytable)

In order to discuss testing for independence of two categorical variables, we need to introduce some notation.

Consider two categorical variables \(A\) and \(B\) with \(I\) and \(J\) levels, respectively. We use \(A_1,A_2,...,A_I,B_1,B_2,...,B_J\) to denote the levels of \(A\) and \(B\),respectively.

In this case, we can let \(A:\) Education with levels 1,2,3,4,5 for the levels of education and \(B:\) GunLaw with levels 1,2 representing “Favor” and “Oppose”, respectively.

From a sample of \(n\) randomly selected individuals, let the random variable \(n_{ij}\) denote the number of observations in the \(i^{th}\) level of \(A\) and the \(j^{th}\) level of \(B\), respectively, \(i\in\{1,2,...,I\},j\in\{1,2,...,J}\).

The probability that a randomly selected individual from the population is classified by the \(i^{th}\) level of \(A\) and the \(j^{th}\) level of \(B\), is denoted \(p_{ij}\).

The \(N_{ij}\) are multinomial random variables with expected value \(E[N_{ij}]=np_{ij}\).

The Hypotheses

\(H_0:\) Opinion on gun safety laws is independent of level of education

\(H_a:\) Opinion on gun safety laws depends on level of education
